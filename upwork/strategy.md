# Upwork Strategy

**[STUB — populate with your own content. See POPULATED BY at the end.]**

## What belongs here

### Current targeting
Contract and project types you're actively pursuing on Upwork — role types, scope patterns, client types.

### Pay floor / rate posture
Minimum rate you'll consider, ideal rate, how you position rate in proposals.

### Hard dealbreakers (used by the job search prompt)
The job search prompt filters against these. Be specific.

Examples of the kind of thing that goes here:
- PhD required → skip
- On-site only → skip
- Fixed-price under $X → skip
- Vague scope / "help with my project" with no specifics → skip
- Listings posted > 14 days ago → skip

### Longer-term positioning
Where you're trying to move — higher-ticket contracts, different niche, repeat clients, etc. This shapes which listings count as "strategic" (high priority) vs. "fine" (medium).

### Signals of a promising listing
What makes you upgrade a job to `high` priority. Examples:
- Client has prior repeat-hire history
- Listing names a problem you've solved in a portfolio item
- Scope is well-defined

### Signals of a weak listing
Short of a hard dealbreaker but reason to downgrade or skip.
