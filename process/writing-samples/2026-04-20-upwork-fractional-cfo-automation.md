perspective and non-technical background makes me a uniquely qualified
candidate to design the kind of linear, efficient automation you're looking
for. You want automations that are simple, clear, and predictable -- more

pipeline.

I built a similar system for a personal health data project -- sensor data
in, automated processing pipeline, structured Notion database with 67
properties, LLM-generated summaries. While it was a little more complex,

On your three questions:

Similar system: see above. I used a FastAPI backend to populate a Notion
database, but Zapier makes a lot more sense for your use case. The real
task for your workflows lies in creating structures in Notion that can feed
into each separate pipeline without mingling the workflows.

How I'd keep it from breaking: Zapier is the right solution -- it provides
the right depth of function but keeps things simple enough that you don't
need a computer science degree to troubleshoot your own workflow. My
documentation will give you clear steps for basic troubleshooting. My goal
would be that anything you couldn't fix within 10 minutes, you could bring
to a Claude session and use one of my provided troubleshooting prompts to
get a diagnosis and steps for a quick fix.

What I would not automate: human review on every client-facing deliverable.
For each workflow, outputs could be packaged for a simple review session
before delivery. The LLM can prompt you item by item for approvals and
revisions with all the pertinent details in one place -- 3 bursts of
decision-making instead of constant oversight.
