I came into AI workflow consulting from the non-technical side, which is
exactly where your team is starting. I learned to use tools like ChatGPT and
Perplexity the same way they will need to learn them — by actually figuring
out which tasks they're helpful for and which tasks are better left to humans.

Before transitioning into healthcare, I spent years in hospitality and F&B,
so I have a concrete sense of how your staff's day-to-day actually looks and
where LLMs can genuinely help rather than add noise.

Most of my workshop experience has been in adjacent areas: team-building,
group communication skills, clinical case presentations, and ongoing project
coordination. I've led many remote and in-person sessions built around a
simple Tell-Show-Do model: first I explain what we're about to learn, then I
demonstrate how I do it, then I support participants as they practice it
themselves.

For your team, I'd adapt that into a modular structure that starts with
participants identifying real goals from their own roles, gives a brief,
practical overview of what LLMs can and can't do (including safety and privacy
considerations), and then moves into hands-on segments where they use LLMs on
their own laptops to work through actual goal-specific tasks — starting with
small chat-based tasks and moving into more powerful generative and agentic
capabilities as appropriate.

For participants who are skeptical or intimidated, I make a point of
validating those reactions and emphasizing that they stay in control of their
work when using LLMs, then guide them through low-friction entry points like
voice input.

To customize this for you, I'd want a few specifics ahead of time: examples
of typical tasks across roles, general sentiment about LLM use and training
among the group, whether all participants will have laptops and real work
material available, and whether this is an opt-in group that's already curious
about AI or a team that's been told they need this training.

I've facilitated hybrid sessions where some participants were together in a
room and others joined remotely; for this kind of hybrid workshop, I'd plan
structured activities that keep remote participants actively involved, likely
having everyone in Zoom for the hands-on portions so they're on the same
footing. I may also use some friendly competition between the in-person and
remote groups to motivate everyone to get the most out of our time together.

I don't have portfolio items specifically from my facilitation work, but I've
attached my portfolio items so you can see how I prepare deliverable
materials, structure AI-related content, and explain complex material clearly.

For this project, I'd propose a flat $1,200 for the half-day workshop. For
optional 1:1 follow-up sessions, my rate is $65/hour, billed in 60-minute
blocks; if team members only want a 30-minute check-in, that's fine — I'll
still reserve and bill for the full hour so we have that time available if
the conversation needs it.

I'd be happy to discuss the workshop in a short call so you can get a direct
sense of fit.
