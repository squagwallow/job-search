Hi -- I see this project as fundamentally about turning a rich text document
into a relational knowledge base, not just moving content out of Word.

A 600-page Bible study with a glossary needs a structure where study notes
and glossary terms are distinct but interoperable, links follow a consistent
visual language, definition updates stay consistent across the system, and
search is optimized for real use. In Notion, I'd likely use a central
Glossary database with Relation properties connecting each chapter or study
section to its terms, plus linked views that act like dynamic reference
lists at the section level.

I'm also comfortable with Obsidian and think it could work well here too,
especially for Markdown-first linking, though Notion generally has more
user-friendly high-level database tools. My instinct would be that Notion
will be the winner for you, but we should investigate that as the first
decision point during the audit. I'd want to review the document structure
first, then compare some buildout schemas with Obsidian and Notion. I'd
also suggest that you consider adding an LLM orchestration component -- this
is purely optional and not a huge amount of extra work, but it might have
some nice benefits we could discuss. Once we choose a structure that works
for you, I'd build a small prototype for you to evaluate, then migrate 10
or so pages, and let you evaluate again. I'd prefer to start working in
live updating chunks so you can test as we go.

I also happen to have a strong Bible background, so I'll have genuine
context for the material and for how you'd expect a study system like this
to function in practice.

Take a look at my case studies on Notion and LLM orchestration, and let me
know if you'd like to schedule a call to discuss further.

Thanks,
Jonathan
