My approach to designing AI-integrated system architecture starts with
processes and workflow needs -- not tools.

Most systems evolve organically over time. Ad hoc integrations accumulate,
and while they may work initially, they often lead to fragmentation,
bottlenecks, and operational drag. In many cases, workflows become dependent
on a single user's knowledge or credentials, documentation is inconsistent,
and AI tools require constant human mediation due to poorly structured data
flow.

To redesign how your work gets done, I begin with a comprehensive audit of
your processes and data movement. From there, I design a system architecture
that aligns your workflows with the right tools, integrations, and automation
layers. Once approved, I handle implementation and deployment, working closely
with your team to ensure a smooth and sustainable transition.

I recently built Cornflower Health, an AI-assisted workflow system that
replaced a fragmented, user-dependent process with a structured, automated
pipeline. The key wasn't just the tools -- it was rethinking the architecture.
I implemented a documentation layer (GitHub as a source of truth) that
orchestrates data from a wearable device into a managed database (Notion),
allowing an LLM (Claude) to read from and write to the system while
preserving context across sessions and users. This transforms AI from a
one-off assistant into a persistent operational layer.

If you would like to discuss how I could help further, I'd love to know more
about what your company does and some of the core tools that make your work
possible.

Thanks,
Jonathan
