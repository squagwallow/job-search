One real example of Claude Code/MCP integration solving a business problem:

Cornflower Health is a working health data pipeline I've been iterating on
for months: Apple Watch data in, FastAPI processing on Render, Notion as
the structured data store (67 properties, 220+ records), Claude as the
synthesis and coaching layer, MCP integrations handling the handoffs. Zero
manual steps. I also built Spinwheel, an LLM-orchestrated task management
system with Notion and GitHub integrations -- closer to what your developer
would use day-to-day. See my case studies in my portfolio for more details.

What I'd do first if I joined a 12-person marketing agency that just gave
everyone Claude access:

I'd build a 10-minute survey for your entire staff: What is the simplest
thing you have used an LLM to do at work? What is the most complex? What
is something you think LLM could help you with but you don't know where to
start? Have you ever tried to get LLM to help with something but it went
wrong or you gave up? What are your five most repetitive tasks, where do
you lose the most time, what have you already tried.

Then I'd identify key leaders whose top-down process needs should frame any
large-scale architecture design, and find your two or three most advanced
LLM users. The most high-value LLM implementations don't require every role
to have advanced understanding, and team-wide wins from your most enthusiastic
people do a lot to boost sentiment around your rollout.

Claude to web) is exactly the kind of high-visibility win I'd prioritize
early. Your developer and I could move fast on that together.
