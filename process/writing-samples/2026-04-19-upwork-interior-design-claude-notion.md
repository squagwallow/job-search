Your framing that you want Claude expertise that accounts for your agency's
actual culture and workflows, not just technical implementation, is very well
matched with my strengths as a system builder.

The first thing I'd focus on is building a documentation system that works
for your specific team. Based on your overview, that's likely a hybrid of
Make.com orchestration, Claude Projects, and Notion databases. The first
major upgrade we could unlock for your staff is creating persistent contexts
with the right scope and access. This would give your team a clear process
for starting a Claude (Code, Chat, or Cowork) thread that has context and
access to client files, design documents, or project statuses. The goal is
rebuilding your operational system in a way that's ready to connect with
orchestration in controlled, intelligent ways -- not creating a massive
clockwork of automations so complicated no one understands what's happening
on the backend.

This is how I work myself. Claude is part of my actual workflow for grad
school, contract work, and research. I specialize in documentation, system
architecture design, and systemic thinking. I've built working systems with
Claude as the orchestration layer -- Notion databases, automated pipelines,
structured outputs from messy inputs -- and you can see those case studies
in my portfolio.

Once that core structure is in place, your team can direct Claude interactions
and choose which data and context is active, with confidence about where the
source of truth lives and what the model is actually pulling from.

Here's my proposal: let's start with one focused discovery session -- 90 to
120 minutes. I'd want to look at your current processes, how work gets done
day to day, and explore how you're already using Claude, Notion, and any
other tools. Then I'd prepare an audit and system architecture document that
includes a prioritized map of what we can build, clear bottlenecks and
logjams, opportunities to take non-critical workflow tasks off the founders'
plates, and immediate next steps.

If the process and report feel right, we scope the build as a second
engagement. If not, you walk away with a high-quality deliverable you can
hand to a different implementor without starting from scratch.

I'd expect the discovery session and audit deliverable to involve no more
than 6 billable hours at my rate of $85/hr. I'd love the opportunity to
discuss further.

Thanks,
