# Notion Proposal Skeleton

[SWAP A: One sentence on their specific problem. What they are migrating from,
or what is broken. Pulled directly from their listing. No generic opener.]

I've been building Notion systems for operational use for a couple of years.
[SWAP B: One sentence portfolio hook. Which project and why it fits
this client specifically. Use a pre-written hook from below, not a new one.]

My approach would be to [SWAP C: Two sentences on how you would actually do it,
specific to what they described. Audit first, architecture before build,
training as you go rather than at the end.]

One question before I put together a proposal: [SWAP D: One specific question
about their setup that you actually need answered to scope this right.]

Jonathan

---

## Pre-written portfolio hooks

Use one. Do not use both. Do not write a new one from scratch.

**Cornflower Health** (health tech, data pipeline, automation-heavy clients):
"Cornflower Health is an AI-directed health data pipeline I designed and have
been running for over a year. It pulls Apple Watch data through a webhook
backend, normalizes it into a structured schema, and syncs into Notion
dashboards with an LLM coaching layer on top. Notion is the operational core
the whole system runs on."

**Job-search system** (multi-database, workflow ops, team workspace clients):
"I've been running a Notion-based project operations system of my own design
for the past several months. It has a live job queue, a writing samples
catalog, session handoff protocols, and GitHub integration for version
control across multiple active use cases."

---

## Swap zone rules

A: Must come from the listing verbatim or near-verbatim. If you are
   generalizing, you are doing it wrong.

B: Pick the pre-written hook above. Swap in one bridging clause if the
   client's domain is specific (e.g. "production company" instead of "project").
   Do not rewrite the hook from scratch.

C: Two sentences max. Name their actual tools if they listed any.
   If they listed none, keep it process-level: audit, architecture, build,
   train. Do not describe a generic Notion setup.

D: Ask the one thing you actually need to know to scope this. Not a warm
   question. A real one. Examples: what is your current team size, what tools
   are you migrating from, do you have existing automation in place.

---

## What this skeleton does not cover

Jobs that require live MCP setup, Cowork integration, or connector debugging.
Those are not Notion workspace builder jobs. Use the full prepare-application
process for those.

Jobs where the client's scope is ambiguous. Spend 60 seconds checking
before using this template. If you cannot fill swap A from their listing,
the listing is too vague to propose on.
