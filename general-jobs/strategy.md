# General-Jobs Strategy — Jonathan Weinisch

---

## Constraints & Context

- **Current status:** Final-year MA student in Couple and Family Therapy at CU Denver. Active clinical practicum with supervised caseload.
- **Availability:** Part-time only (~15–25 hrs/week max). Practicum schedule is non-negotiable. Target start: immediate.
- **Timeline:** MA completion expected end of 2026. LMFT licensure track requires 2,000 supervised hours post-graduation. Job search now is for bridge/concurrent income, not a career pivot.
- **Schedule constraints:** Daytime hours partially committed to practicum. Evenings and flexible/async roles strongly preferred.
- **No relocation:** Denver Metro area only for in-person. Remote is fully open.

## Target Pay

| Format | Target | Minimum Acceptable |
|---|---|---|
| Hourly (contract/part-time) | $25–$55/hr | $20/hr |
| Salaried part-time (W2) | $35,000–$55,000 FTE-equivalent | $28,000 prorated |
| AI trainer / evaluator (contract) | $35–$65/hr | $25/hr |
| Health / medical writing (freelance) | $25–$45/hr | $20/hr |

**Salary floor rationale:** Below $20/hr at part-time hours does not cover costs and conflicts with the value of practicum time. Roles below this floor are skipped unless they provide supervised clinical hours toward LMFT licensure.

**Exception:** Roles offering free or subsidized clinical supervision hours toward LMFT licensure (2,000 hrs required post-MA) may be worth accepting below the pay floor — evaluate case-by-case.

## Role Clusters — Priority Order

### Tier 1 — Highest Priority

#### A. Group Facilitator / Behavioral Health Facilitator (Remote or Hybrid)

**Why:** Direct match to clinical training, facilitation certification, and current practicum work. Charlie Health ($54–$57/hr in Denver) represents the top of this market. High hourly rate, remote-friendly, part-time-compatible.

**Market reality:** ~60+ open pre-licensed/facilitator roles in Denver Metro. Remote group facilitator pay ranges $19–$57/hr depending on licensure level and organization type. Charlie Health is an outlier on the high end.

**Charlie Health note:** Three active applications already submitted. Reviews are mixed (2.7–3.0/5 on management and culture)[web:20] but pay is well above market[web:25][web:26]. Worth pursuing aggressively — but pipeline has stalled; consider direct recruiter outreach if no response within 2 weeks.

**Target employers:** Charlie Health, Manatee, Brightline, Hazel Health, Headspace Health, Denver-area IOP/PHP programs, NAMI Colorado, Recovery centers with group therapy programs.

**Search terms:** "group facilitator" mental health remote, "psychoeducational facilitator", "behavioral health group facilitator", "IOP group therapist pre-licensed", "group counselor intern"

---

#### B. AI Trainer / Evaluator / Response Rater (Remote, Contract)

**Why:** Directly leverages AI background from Upwork track (LLM prompt engineering, clinical AI case study) plus behavioral health domain expertise — a rare combination that commands premium rates. Fully async, fully remote, no schedule conflict with practicum.

**Market reality:** AI trainer/evaluator market is wide but noisy. Domain experts (mental health, clinical, public health) command $35–$150/hr[web:24] vs. general raters at $8–$30/hr[web:13]. The clinical/mental health domain is explicitly listed as premium by platforms like Mercor, NeuraScale, Braintrust. Specialization is the differentiator here.

**Target platforms/employers:** Scale AI, Outlier, Appen, Remotasks, YO HR Consultancy (already applied), RPM Coaching (already applied via Indeed), Mercor, NeuraScale, Prolific, Braintrust AI, Surge AI.

**Positioning:** Lead with clinical internship + MFT training as the domain credential. The combination of clinical knowledge AND demonstrated AI/LLM experience (Cornflower Health, clinical failure-mode case study) is a strong differentiator — most applicants have one or the other, not both.

**Search terms:** "AI trainer mental health", "AI evaluator clinical", "response rater psychology", "RLHF domain expert health", "AI data annotator therapist"

### Tier 2 — Strong Match, Apply Selectively

#### C. Pre-Licensed / Post-Masters Therapist (Part-Time, W2 or Contract)

**Why:** Clinical hours count toward LMFT licensure (2,000 supervised hours required)[web:30][web:33]. Even at lower pay, positions that provide supervision hours have compounding value. Need to verify each employer's supervision structure.

**Market reality:** 36–159 open pre-licensed therapist roles in Colorado/Denver[web:6][web:41]. Pay ranges $28–$70/hr for part-time. Some employers (Khesed Wellness, Heartland Mental Health, Lumen Therapy) offer supervision support as part of compensation.

**Hard constraint:** Role must be part-time compatible with active CU Denver practicum. Cannot take a position requiring 22+ hrs/week of client sessions (e.g., Lumen Therapy's minimum) without practicum conflict.

**Target employers:** Khesed Wellness, Heartland Mental Health, Denver Children's Home, iBelong Counseling, community mental health centers, IOP programs, telehealth platforms (Talkspace, BetterHelp — note: supervision quality varies).

**Search terms:** "pre-licensed therapist part-time", "post-masters clinician", "associate therapist supervision", "MFT intern paid", "telehealth therapist pre-licensed Colorado"

---

#### D. Health / Public Health Writer (Remote, Freelance or Part-Time)

**Why:** Directly maps to Doxastic background (4 yrs science writing, global health policy) + MA research training. Low barrier to entry for content/health writing vs. clinical medical writing. Fully async, schedule-compatible.

**Market reality:** Entry-level remote health content writing pays $20–$32/page or $22–$45/hr[web:19]. Medical writing (clinical, regulatory) pays significantly more ($84k–$95k/yr entry) but typically requires pharma/clinical trial experience not in the profile. Target the content/public health writing tier, not pharma/regulatory medical writing.

**Realistic targets:** Health content agencies, patient education organizations, public health nonprofits, health journalism/media outlets, science communication roles. Avoid: pharma regulatory writing, clinical trial documentation (overqualified in domain, underqualified in industry experience).

**Search terms:** "health content writer remote", "public health writer freelance", "science writer part-time", "patient education writer", "behavioral health content creator", "mental health blog writer"

#### E. Research Assistant — Behavioral Health / Psychology (Remote, Part-Time)

**Why:** Matches academic/research background and current CU context. Often university-affiliated with flexible hours. Typical pay $18–$25/hr[web:35] — lower end but schedule-compatible and CV-building.

**Market reality:** Limited volume of remote positions at this level. Most university RA roles are in-person or geography-locked. Better returns on time spent applying to Tier 1 clusters.

**Best sources:** CU Denver/Boulder job boards, Indeed, university research lab websites, SAMHSA-funded study postings.

**Search terms:** "research assistant behavioral health remote", "research coordinator mental health part-time", "study coordinator psychology"

## Hard Dealbreakers — Auto-Skip

- Full-time hours required (40 hrs/week) — conflicts with practicum
- PhD or doctoral degree required
- Active LMFT/LPC/LCSW licensure required (pre-licensed roles only)
- Security clearance required
- Pay below $20/hr (unless supervision hours provided — evaluate case-by-case)
- Relocation required outside Denver Metro
- On-site only outside Denver Metro
- Commission-only compensation
- Roles requiring 2+ years post-licensure clinical experience

## Signals of a Strong Listing

- Explicitly says "pre-licensed", "associate", "intern", or "post-masters"
- Mentions supervision hours or clinical supervision support
- Part-time, flexible schedule, or async-compatible
- Remote or Denver Metro hybrid
- Behavioral health, group facilitation, family systems, or public health focus
- AI/mental health intersection (evaluator, trainer, domain expert)
- Mission-driven org (nonprofit, community health, IOP, university-affiliated)
- Pay listed and above $20/hr

---

## Signals of a Weak Listing (Downgrade, Don't Auto-Skip)

- Pay not listed (research before skipping — some BH orgs hide competitive pay)
- "Case manager" in title without clinical focus (may still count toward hours)
- Large telehealth platform (supervision quality varies widely — check reviews)
- "Group facilitator" at a wellness/coaching org with no clinical framework (lower pay, no licensure value)
- Charlie Health (high pay but mixed culture reviews — worth applying but temper expectations)

## LMFT Licensure Strategy Note

Colorado requires 2,000 supervised clinical hours post-MA for LMFT licensure[web:30][web:33]. Any paid role that accumulates board-approved supervision hours has value beyond its hourly rate. When evaluating pre-licensed therapist roles:

1. Confirm the employer provides board-approved supervision (ask explicitly)
2. Confirm hours will count toward Colorado LMFT requirement
3. Weigh below-floor pay against supervision hour value — at 10 hrs/week, a role providing supervision could accelerate licensure by months, which has real downstream income value
